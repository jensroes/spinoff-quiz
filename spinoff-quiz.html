Choosing a statistical test

You're a stats consultant and need to advise colleagues on which test to use:

p-value

At the core of inferential statistics is the p-value. The p-value is a probability, i.e. a number between 0 and 1 where 0 is ‘never’ and 1 is ‘always’. This probability stands for the likelihood of an event. This event has to do with the data we observe and the null hypothesis H\(_0\). Explore the meaning in the following questions.


Because of the inverse logic of what the p-value means, there is a lot of confusion, even among researchers, about what the p-value means. So here is your chance to demonstrate you know it better :)

There is a difference between what the test statistic means and how we use the test statistic to draw conclusions about our hypothesis.
